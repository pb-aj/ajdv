Un-spot-able Signals:
Searching for Null Space Radial Velocity

A.J. deVaux1, Emily Rauscher1, Rachael Roettenbacher1

1 University of Michigan

Contact: ajdv@umich.edu

Abstract

Without knowing the mass of a terrestrial exoplanet, our understanding of it will be very limited. This makes accurately measuring the radial velocity (RV) of its host star crucial. One issue with accomplishing this is that stars generate other sources of RV, such as RV signals induced by starspots. Fortunately, if we can identify and map these starspots photometrically, then we can account for their added signals and remove them from our measurements. But, on every star there exists patterns that contribute no photometric signal in stellar rotation curves called the null space. We have determined that these “unspottable” patterns do induce RV signals, much like starspots, meaning the null space could add another complicating factor to exoplanet mass measurements. We are searching for the significance of these invisible signals by creating a mathematical framework that identifies spatial patterns that can exist in a star’s null space. Using these patterns as “null space” maps, we calculate the RV signals they induce for physically reasonable flux variations on the stellar surface. We will present our results from these models and discuss the future impacts that the null space will have on detecting exoplanets using RV.
